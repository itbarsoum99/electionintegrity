The Election Handbook

The Election Handbook provides a nonpartisan voter guide for states that receive a grade of C+ or worse on the Election Transparency Index. In these states, it is considered more difficult than average to vote. There is also a general voter guide for states where it is easier than the US average to vote.

Alabama

    To register:
  • Eligible adults must register before October 20.
    • To vote by mail:
  • No excuse needed.
  • You must request an absentee ballot to vote.
  • A photo ID must be submitted vith the application to vote by mail.
    • To vote in person:
  • A photo ID or affidavit attesting to your identity must be presented to vote.

Georgia

    To register:
  • Eligible adults must register before October 5.
    • To vote by mail:
  • No excuse needed.
  • You must request an absentee ballot to vote.
    • To vote in person:
  • A photo ID is strictly required to vote.

Idaho

    To register:
  • Eligible adults must register before October 9.
    • To vote by mail:
  • No excuse needed.
  • You must request an absentee ballot to vote.
    • To vote in person:
  • A photo ID or affidavit attesting to your identity must be presented to vote.

Indiana

    To register:
  • Eligible adults must register by October 5.
    • To vote by mail:
  • Must have an excuse other than COVID-19.
  • You must request an application to receive an absentee ballot to vote.
    • To vote in person:
  • A photo ID is strictly required to vote.
