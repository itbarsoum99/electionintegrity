The Election Handbook

The Election Handbook is providing a guide to where the candidates stand on the issues. These are simply the claims each candidate makes on their respective website. They are not guarantees, and they have not been meticulously fact-checked.

Jump to an issue: COVID-19 | Race in America | Climate Change | Healthcare | The Economy

COVID-19

Biden says that he would:

  • Implement widespread testing-and-tracing, by setting up a pandemic testing board to guarantee regular, reliable, and free access to testing for all, double the number of drive-through testing sites, and build a national contact tracing workforce.
  • Establish a sustainable PPE supply chain by utilizing the Defense Production Act, replenishing depleted supplies in hard-hit areas and "build now toward a future, flexible American-sourced and manufactured capability to ensure we are not vulnerable to supply chain disruptions in times of crisis."
  • Accelerate a coordinated global approach for a safe and effective vaccine by increasing manufacturing of candidates, protecting research from cyberattacks, proactively building a nationwide vaccine campaign, restoring our relationship with the WHO, and building a clinical trial network to bring scientific talent together.
  • Create a stronger, more effective reopening by delivering a clear, consistent message on mask-wearing, increasing testing and contact tracing, guaranteeing COVID-19 paid leave, setting an emergency temporary standard for employers, equipping businesses with COVID-19 certifications and a "restart package," and assisting schools with re-opening.
  • Protect older Americans by ensuring evidence-based guidance is provided, creating more testing capacity so that visitors can be accommodated, and boosting Social Security payments to ensure that people on fixed incomes can "weather the long arc of the pandemic."

Trump issued "Coronavirus Guidelines for America" in March, which suggested preventative measures. They include:

  • If you or someone in your household is sick, stay home and keep them home.
  • If you are an older person, stay home.
  • If you have underlying health conditions, stay home.
  • Listen to and follow the directions of your state and local authorities.
  • Engage in work and school from home whenever possible.
  • Follow CDC guidelines in the workplace.
  • Avoid social gatherings of more than 10 people.
  • Avoid eating or drinking at bars, restaurants, and food courts.
  • Avoid discretionary travel.
  • Do not visit nursing homes or retirement communities.
  • Practice good hygiene by washing your hands, avoiding touching your face, sneezing into a tissue or the inside of your elbow, and disinfecting frequently used items and surfaces as much as possible.

These guidelines do not include to wear a mask, the scientific consensus on which was reached after they were released. The president has imposed no mandates on any of these guidelines, though states have imposed mandates for some or all of them.

Race in America

Biden’s plan for racial economic equity, as laid out on his website is to:

  • Spur Public-Private Investment through a New Small Business Opportunity Plan by giving more than $50 billion in venture capital to Black and Brown entrepreneurs.
  • Reform Opportunity Zones to Fulfill Their Promise by incentivizing Opportunity Funds to partner with non-profit community organizations to focus on creating jobs for low-income residents.
  • Make a Historic Commitment to Equalizing Federal Procurement by creating American jobs and "unleashing new demand for domestic goods and services" to benefit small disadvantaged businesses.
  • Ensure His Housing Plan Makes Bold Investments in Homeownership and Access to Affordable Housing for Black, Brown, and Native Families by helping these families buy their first homes, scaling up support for investing in homeownership in revitalized areas, and constructing public housing to address the affordable housing crisis.
  • Achieve Equity in Management, Training, and Higher Education Opportunities Connected to the Jobs of the Future by providing relief from student debt; doubling the maximum value of Pell grants; cracking down on private lenders; forgiving all undergraduate tuition-related federal student debt for public colleges, and making public colleges free for families with incomes below $125,000; and providing support for HBCUs; making an investment in workforce training, and requiring public companies to disclose diversity data for their boards.
  • Boost Retirement Security and Financial Wealth for Black, Brown, and Native Families by equalizing tax benefits of defined contribution plans, and giving small businesses a tax break for starting a retirement plan.
  • Ensure Workers of Color Are Compensated Fairly and Treated With Dignity by ending pay discrimination and increasing the federal minimum wage to $15/hour, preventing overtime pay denial, addressing discrimination in the workplace, guaranteeing family and sick leave, and making it easier for all workers to organize unions.
  • Ensure Equity in Biden’s Bold Infrastructure and Clean Energy Investments by rebuilding thoroughfares, redoing lighting, repairing sidewalks, building parks, adding school A/C, investing in facilities for victims of crime, incentivizing supermarkets to bring fresh foods to communities, and ensuring public infrastructure is fully accessible.
  • Support Second Chances for Economic Success by expanding access to mental health treatment, eliminating barriers keeping formerly incarcerated individuals from participating in society, and eliminating cash bail.
  • Strengthen the Federal Reserve’s Focus on Racial Economic Gaps by promoting diverse leadership for all federal agencies and ensuring that federal dollars go to high-poverty areas.
  • Build a 21st Century Care Infrastructure by expanding access to Medicaid, child care/preschool, and building safe child care facilities.
  • Address Longstanding Inequities in Agriculture by establishing an Equity Commission and expanding protections for farm workers.

Biden’s plan for reforming criminal justice includes:

  • Reducing incarceration and reducing crime.
  • Eliminating racial, gender, and income-based disparities in the system.
  • Focus on redemption and rehabilitation.
  • Prevent profiteering off of the justice system.
  • Offering second chances.
  • Reducing violence in our communities.

Trump’s "Platinum Plan for Black Americans" includes:

  • Creating 3 million new jobs for the Black community.
  • Creating 500,000 new Black owned businesses.
  • Increase access to capital in Black communities by almost $500 billion.
  • Create safe urban neighborhoods with "highest policing standards."
  • Commit to working on a Second Step Act.
  • Creating access to better education and job training opportunities.
  • Giving Black churches the ability to compete for federal resources for their community.
  • Bringing better and tailored healthcare to address historic disparities.
  • Utilizing an immigration policy that "protects American jobs".
  • Advancing home ownership opportunities and enhancing financial literacy in the Black community.
  • On-shoring manufacturing to advance jobs and develop opportunities for Black-owned businesses.

Climate Change

Biden’s plan for Climate Change includes making far-reaching investments in:

  • Infrastructure: Create millions of good, union jobs rebuilding America’s crumbling infrastructure – from roads and bridges to green spaces and water systems to electricity grids and universal broadband – to lay a new foundation for sustainable growth, compete in the global economy, withstand the impacts of climate change, and improve public health, including access to clean air and clean water.
  • Auto Industry: Create 1 million new jobs in the American auto industry, domestic auto supply chains, and auto infrastructure, from parts to materials to electric vehicle charging stations, positioning American auto workers and manufacturers to win the 21st century; and invest in U.S. auto workers to ensure their jobs are good jobs with a choice to join a union.
  • Transit: Provide every American city with 100,000 or more residents with high-quality, zero-emissions public transportation options through flexible federal investments with strong labor protections that create good, union jobs and meet the needs of these cities – ranging from light rail networks to improving existing transit and bus lines to installing infrastructure for pedestrians and bicyclists.
  • Power Sector: Move ambitiously to generate clean, American-made electricity to achieve a carbon pollution-free power sector by 2035. This will enable us to meet the existential threat of climate change while creating millions of jobs with a choice to join a union.
  • Buildings: Upgrade 4 million buildings and weatherize 2 million homes over 4 years, creating at least 1 million good-paying jobs with a choice to join a union; and also spur the building retrofit and efficient-appliance manufacturing supply chain by funding direct cash rebates and low-cost financing to upgrade and electrify home appliances and install more efficient windows, which will cut residential energy bills.
  • Housing: Spur the construction of 1.5 million sustainable homes and housing units.
  • Innovation: Drive dramatic cost reductions in critical clean energy technologies, including battery storage, negative emissions technologies, the next generation of building materials, renewable hydrogen, and advanced nuclear – and rapidly commercialize them, ensuring that those new technologies are made in America.
  • Agriculture and Conservation: Create jobs in climate-smart agriculture, resilience, and conservation, including 250,000 jobs plugging abandoned oil and natural gas wells and reclaiming abandoned coal, hardrock, and uranium mines – providing good work with a choice to join or continue membership in a union in hard-hit communities, including rural communities, reducing leakage of toxins, and preventing local environmental damage.
  • Environmental Justice: Ensure that environmental justice is a key consideration in where, how, and with whom we build – creating good, union, middle-class jobs in communities left behind, righting wrongs in communities that bear the brunt of pollution, and lifting up the best ideas from across our great nation – rural, urban, and tribal.

Trump has declared that global warming was created "by and for the Chinese in order to make U.S. manufacturing non-competitive." However, it is the overwhelming scientific consensus that global warming is real. Trump’s policies, however, don’t reflect this. According to the New York Times, during his administration, nearly 100 climate rollbacks have occurred, including:

  • Left the Paris Climate Agreement in which all signatories pledged to cut greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Weakened fuel economy and greenhouse gas standards for some cars and trucks.
  • Replaced the Clean Power Plan, which set strict limits on carbon emissions from coal and gas power plants, with a version that lets states set their own rules.
  • Eliminated a requirement for oil and gas companies to report methane emissions.
  • Eliminated a rule designed to limit toxic emissions from major industrial polluters.
  • Eliminated an Obama executive order that set a 40% greenhouse gas reduction goal for the federal government.
  • Creating a plan to open up part of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge in Alaska for oil and gas development.
  • Replaced an executive order that preserved major waters with a policy focused on energy production and economic growth.
  • Reduced the environmental review process for federal infrastructure projects.
  • Scaled back pollution protections for certain tributaries and wetlands.
  • Rejected a proposed ban on chlorpyrifos, a pesticide linked to developmental disabilities in children.
  • Ended payments to the Green Climate Fund, a UN program to help poorer countries reduce carbon emissions.

Healthcare

Biden has proposed protecting and building on the ACA, through these vital steps:

  • Giving every American access to affordable insurance with a public option system like Medicare, increasing the value of tax credits to lower premiums, and expanding coverage to low-income Americans.
  • Providing peace of mind with a less complex system, including a new, more affordable option, a premium tax credit for middle-class families, the end of surprise medical billing, using antitrust authority to end healthcare market concentration, and lowering costs by partnering with the health care workforce.
  • Standing up to abuse of power by pharma companies by repealing the Medicare exception that allows drug corporations to avoid negotiating with Medicare, limiting launch prices for drugs that have no competition, limiting price increases for drugs to inflation allowing patients to buy prescription drugs from other countries ending pharma tax breaks, and improving the supply of quality generics.
  • Ensuring healthcare is a right for all by expanding access to abortion through restoring funding for Planned Parenthood and reversing the assault on the right to choose; reducing high maternal mortality rate; defending healthcare protections for all; doubling America’s investment in community health centers; and expanding access to mental health care.

Trump, on the other hand, is fighting to end the Affordable Care Act, an action that could cause 20 million Americans to lose their health coverage. In his plan, Trump says that he:

  • Has taken numerous actions to increase choice and competition in health care to bring down costs, through expanding the use of short-term, limited-duration plans, which offer more choices to consumers; expanding access to Association Health Plans to make it easier for small businesses to band together to offer better insurance; and to expand health reimbursement arrangements for employers.
  • Has taken actions to dismantle Obamacare, which has lowered Obamacare premiums, through tax cuts, which repealed the individual mandate; and through repealing the independent payment advisory board. He says that "over the last two years, premiums for Obamacare plans are declining for the first time."
  • Is working to protect the most vulnerable in our healthcare system, including being committed to protecting Americans with pre-existing conditions; giving terminally ill patients "hope and the ability to try new experimental drugs"; signing the Childhood Cancer STAR Act, investing millions for child cancer research; and taking action to expand the availability of organ donations.
  • Is working to set ambitious healthcare goals, including putting forward principles to stop surprise billing; and setting a goal to
    end the HIV/AIDS epidemic in the US within a decade.
  • Is fighting to bring down drug prices by approving record numbers of generic drugs; introducing new principles for reducing prescription drug costs; signing legislation to improve drug pricing transparency; eliminating kickbacks to help Medicare recipients; working to allow the importation of cheaper drugs from other countries; and helping lower-income Americans gain access to life-saving medications.

The Economy

Biden’s "Build Back Better" plan outlines his plan for rebuilding the economy:

  • Mobilize American manufacturing and innovation to "ensure that the future is made in America, and in all of America," through building a strong industrial base and small-business-led supply chains to retain and create millions of good-paying union jobs in manufacturing and technology.
  • Mobilize American ingenuity to "build a modern infrastructure and an equitable, clean energy future" through investing in modern, sustainable infrastructure; meeting the climate crisis; building a clean energy economy; addressing environmental justice; and creating millions of good-paying union jobs.
  • Mobilize American talent and heart to build a 21st-century caregiving and education workforce to help ease the burden of care for working parents through making it easier to afford child care; ensuring aging relatives and people with disabilities have better access to care; to elevate pay and benefits for caregivers and educators; to and to create millions of good-paying new union jobs in these areas.
  • Mobilize to advance racial equity in America by closing the racial wealth gap, expanding affordable housing, investing in minority entrepreneurs and communities, advancing criminal justice reform, and promise educational opportunity "regardless of race or zip code."

Trump’s policy focuses on reducing the trade deficit, eliminating the national debt, and cutting taxes. According to the president, his record includes:

  • Real GDP growth that has beaten the Congressional Budget Office’s projections every year.
  • Adding 5 million new jobs than the CBO projected prior to the 2016 election.
  • The unemployment rate is 1.4 percentage points lower than the CBO’s projection.

These numbers, however, were published four days before the major economic crash due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Current numbers include:

  • According to the BLS, all but three states in the country have hit their historical high in unemployment rates in 2020.
  • The unemployment rate is 7.9% nationally.
  • The GDP shrunk by 31.4% in the second quarter of 2020, after a 5% decrease in the first quarter; two subsequent quarters of GDP decrease means that the US is officially in an economic recession.

The two candidates had a dispute over the economy in the first presidential debate, with Trump saying that the US was in the midst of a V-shaped economic recovery, while Biden said it was K-shaped, with gains going overwhelmingly to the rich.
