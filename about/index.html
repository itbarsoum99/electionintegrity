About Us

The Election Integrity Project is a nonprofit service project created to spread information about the civic responsibility of voting, the process of elections, and the issues upon which those elections are based. All funds raised go to the Common Cause Education Fund, an educational nonprofit that aims to strengthen democracy in the United States. To donate, please visit the Donate page.

Our Mission

The Election Integrity Project aims to spread information to students around the country, in order to prepare this generation for its chance to participate in the political process.

How the Program Works

The 2020 program, which will consist of five one-hour sessions, in which the students and the instructor will discuss political issues, political involvement, and fact-checking. We will help the student develop their own political beliefs free of partisan bias and influence. Our goal is not to influence the minds of the next generation, but to help them cultivate their own beliefs.

Our Founder

The founder of the Election Integrity Project is Isaac Barsoum, a 14-year-old student at an International Baccalaureate high school in North Carolina. Isaac has taken and succeeded in 4 years of Social Studies classes, including Civics/Economics, US History, and World History. He loves politics and news and reads articles in The New York Times and The Washington Post daily. He began this project using his own research and data to form the Election Transparency Index.
