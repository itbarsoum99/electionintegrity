Election Transparency Index

The Election Transparency Index is based on transparency of statistics, mail-in voting, ease of voter registration/election, and photo ID laws. The index takes the average of scores from all four of these categories and converts it into a letter grade for the state. Our data comes from sources including individual states' Secretary of State websites, The Washington Post, and Slate's voting guide for 2020. The scores are intended to be unbiased reflections of the transparency of the election in each US state.

Alabama

C+

Alabama's election practices receive a 79 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. The election statistics for registration are transparent except that there are no specific dates included. There is no excuse needed to vote by mail, but voters must request an application to do. Nobody can register within two weeks of the election, and a photo ID is requested for voting. If voters cannot provide a photo ID, voters must sign an affidavit to prove their identity.

Alaska

B

Alaska's election practices receive an 84 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. There are easy to find and recent voter registration statistics but they are not easy to read. There is no excuse required in order to vote by mail in Alaska but voters must request an application to do so. Voter registration is available in three languages, and a non-photo ID or affidavit is required to vote.

Voter registration is available in English and Spanish. A form of non-photo ID or an affidavit is required to vote.

District of Columbia

A

Washington, D.C.'s election practices receive a 93 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. All voters are automatically sent a mail-in ballot. If there is a ballot defect, voters will be notified and will have the opportunity to fix their ballot. There is no online registration, but interaction with the DC DMV automatically registers eligible adults to vote. Early voting begins October 27. No identification documents are required for voting.

A photo ID is strictly required in order to vote.

Kentucky

B-

Mississippi

F

Mississippi has terrible election practices in every evaluated category. It receives a 14 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. No voter registration statistics are available on any government website. Absentee voters must have a notary to request a ballot and to fill out their ballot, and an excuse to vote by mail beyond COVID-19 is required. There are no early voting sites. Ballots with signature errors will be voided without contacting the voter. A photo ID is strictly required for voting. Overall, Mississippi employs the strongest voter suppression tactics of any state in the nation.

North Carolina

B-

North Carolina's election practices receive an 81 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. North Dakota

D

North Dakota has poor election practices overall. It receives a 65 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. Its seductively suppressive voter registration tactics appear, on the outside, to be a good thing. Unfortunately, their other election practices essentially negate their no-registration-required policy. There is no excuse needed to vote by mail, but voters must request an application to do so. Early voting begins in October. A photo ID is required to vote, and if the voter's date of birth or address is not on the ID, they must also bring a utility bill, paycheck, or government issued document to prove their date of birth or address.

Ohio

C+

Ohio's election practices receive a 78 for transparency, mail-in voting, and ease of voter registration and election. In Ohio, there is no registration data available, so 2016 presidential election records are the best measure of potential voters this year. There is no excuse needed to vote by mail, and an application to do so is automatically sent to voters. There is only one absentee ballot dropbox per county, no matter how many voters there are in that county. On Election Day, there is no same-day registration. Early voting begins in October and varies by county. On election day, there is no same-day registration. To vote, voters must show a photo ID or a non-photo form of identification.
